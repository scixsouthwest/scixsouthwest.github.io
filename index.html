About

Do you like finding out about the world around you? Do you also enjoy fun?

Us too. We believe science is part of culture, and as such should be available, accessible, critiqued and generally not taken too seriously. We run events in Bristol and beyond where audiences can laugh and learn, and performers - even those without science training! - can share their specialist interests in creative ways. Hard to envisage? You can hear our previous events here.

Frequently Asked Questions

I want to come but…

Q) I’m not a scientist and I don’t work in science.

We would PARTICULARLY like you to come. It gets lonely talking to ourselves and boring talking to people who are just like us. If there’s anything you don’t understand, it’s our fault, not yours. Please let us know and we’ll try and improve for next time.

Q) I have access needs.

Unfortunately our regular evening venue has accessibility issues. If you have an idea for a better new place please get in contact! In the meantime we’ll be doing lunchtime tasters at the University of Bristol for people who need stair-free access, travel in the evening, have caring responsibilities, or otherwise find that the Crofter’s Rights doesn’t suit. See events listing for upcoming accessible gigs.



I want to perform but...

Q) I work in [insert human facing thing here]. Is my science science enough?

Social science is science, it’s why it has science in its name. We interpret our remit very broadly, in that any material with some link to things going on outside you is probably valid. In the past, we’ve welcomed talks about sexuality studies, linguistics and technology to bring communities together. If you’re unsure, why not write to us for a chat?

Q) I am not a practicing scientist. I still have something to say about science.

That is fine and we love it.

Q) I’m not a comedian

You don’t have to be. Anything that will hold an audience’s interest for up to 10 minutes is welcome. If you need to set up for instruments, dance, special FX, just contact us and we’ll accommodate if we can.

Q) I am nervous and don’t know if I can do it.

We all had our first time once! We run training sessions to help people planning to dip a toe into the water to plan that experience. Check out when they are on here, or write to us about your ideas and if there’s interest we’ll schedule something.
